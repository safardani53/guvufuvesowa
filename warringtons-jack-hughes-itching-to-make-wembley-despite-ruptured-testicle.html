Warrington's Jack Hughes itching to make Wembley despite ruptured TESTICLE!

 Posted on August 28, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  522 words  |  Martina Birk

NO-ONE will have more balls if they play at Wembley than Jack Hughes – who is itching to make the Challenge Cup final despite suffering a ruptured TESTICLE!

The Warrington co-skipper defied the pain – and the swelling – by playing in the recent Super League match at Catalans for 50 minutes despite the eye watering problem.

Hughes was rushed to a French hospital and operated on that night after the severity of his agony was revealed in scans.

Now he is ready to face St Helens in Saturday’s Wembley showpiece, despite having 12 stitches in his genitals.

“It was probably the size of an orange,” Hughes revealed about the extent of his problem.

“I was told I’d need surgery pretty urgently when I had an ultrasound. So rather than risking it and going home, I got it sorted and gladly I’ve still got two in tact.

“The specialist explained I’d ruptured a third of my testicle, so the rest of it was coming out but he was hopeful he could repair it.

“But I had the chat about what life is like with one testicle, pretty much the same. I was at ease with the worst case scenario.

“However, it’s something serious that can have an impact on your life. If not know, further down the line.

“I remember the moment it happened. We kicked off, they brought it back and I copped a knee – it was one of those pains you wouldn’t wish on your worst enemy.

“I couldn’t get up off my haunches for five minutes then it eased off a bit.

“I checked myself at half-time but 20 minutes into the second half the swelling started to come out. The club doctor couldn’t tell what was what.

“But I’m good. I had my stitches out at the back end of last week and I feel a lot better. They were that tight they were giving me a bit of discomfort.

“Hopefully after a couple of sessions I feel good enough to play.”

Back-rower Hughes, 27, will resume training today in his bid to make Wembley and is prepared to wear extra protection.

One person who definitely will not is St Helens’ Matty Lees, who tore a stomach muscle and needed an operation after facing Leeds on Thursday.
