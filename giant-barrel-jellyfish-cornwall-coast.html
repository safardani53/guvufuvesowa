Giant jellyfish 'as big as a human' spotted by divers off Cornwall coast

 Posted on July 7, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  460 words  |  Aldo Pusey

HUNDREDS of human-sized jellyfish have been spotted lurking around the Cornish coast in the past few days.

Marine experts have said that there have been more sightings of these gigantic creatures this week than in the last 15 years.

Lizzie Daly, a biologist with Wild Ocean Week, said that the animal she came into contact with during a dive in Falmouth was the same size as her.

Barrel jellyfish are the largest species of jellyfish found in British waters, with a diameter of up to 90cm and weights of up to 35 kilos.

This year, hundreds have been washed up on beaches across Cornwall and Devon.

Rupert Kirkwood, who is known as The Lone Kayaker, wrote on his blog earlier this year: “There are more barrel jellyfish around the coast of SW England than I have ever seen in 15-plus years of sea kayaking.

"A lot more. I have seen more in the last three days than all the other years added together … 91 on Tuesday, 120+ on Wednesday and 40+ today.

BARREL JELLYFISH: A GENTLE GIANT

The barrel jellyfish is common off the southern and western coasts of the UK in the summer months and are drawn in by the supplies of plankton.

They are often seen in late spring and wash up on our beaches in May or June – sometimes in their hundreds.

They are the size of dustbin lids - giving them their other common name: Dustbin-lid Jellyfish.

The jellyfish have eight arms, which are frilly in appearance. These frills actually contain their small stinging tentacles which surround hundreds of mouths.

A barrel sting is too weak to cause any serious harm to humans.
