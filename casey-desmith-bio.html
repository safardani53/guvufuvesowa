Casey DeSmith | Early Life, Parents & Education

Casey DeSmith was born to his parents, Gary Desmith and Patrice DeSmith, in Rochester, New Hampshire, on August 13, 1991.

Likewise, Casey grew up with his two sisters, Kylene Desmith and Kirin DeSmith, in a middle-class family in Strafford County.

Unfortunately, there is not much information regarding Desmith’s parents or siblings.

However, what we do know is that Casey played junior hockey in the NEPSAC league for Berwick Academy from 2006 to 2008.

Thereafter, he joined Indian Ice of the United States Hockey League, averaging 2.80 GAA, 0.911 save percentage, and three shutouts in 64 games.

About Desmith’s education, the New Hampshire native attended the University of New Hampshire from 2011 to 2014.

During his time with the Wildcats, Casey turned out to be one of the most consistent players. As a matter of fact, he was even named to the Hockey East All-Rookie in his first year.

Casey DeSmith | Ice Hockey Career

Casey’s professional career started with the Wheeling Nailers in the ECHL. Subsequently, the 28-year-old played just 13 games for the franchise before moving to Wilkes-Barre/Scranton Penguins in the AHL.

During his time with the Penguins, Desmith established himself as one of the most reliable goaltenders in the league.

Moreover, by the time he finished with the Penguins, Casey had played in 62 games, managing three shutouts.

Thus, having performed very well in the AHL, Scranton Penguins‘ NHL affiliate team Pittsburgh Penguins signed the 28-year-old on a one-year two-way contract.

Following solid outings during the chances he was given, the Penguins signed DeSmith on a three-year $3.75 million deal. 

Since then, the New Hampshire native has proved himself to be one of the best substitute goaltenders in the league.

In fact, DeSmith managed a total of 50 games, managing an impressive four shutouts during his last two years with the Penguins.

Added to that, Casey is also an elite professional who never complains about his lack of playing time.

Instead, he relishes the opportunity to play in the NHL and knows his time will come in the future. But, as for now, DeSmith is content with the role he is being given.
