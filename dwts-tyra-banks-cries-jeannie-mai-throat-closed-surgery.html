DANCING With The Stars personality Tyra Banks broke down in tears as former contestant Jeannie Mai opened up about being hospitalized with a life-threatening disease. 

The Real co-host was forced to drop out of the ABC competition on Monday after being rushed to the emergency room for epiglottitis - a rare and dangerous throat condition. 

On tonight’s episode of the reality series, Jeannie, 41, gave DWTS viewers an intimate glimpse at her sudden and scary health situation. 

Tyra, 46, said at the top of the show that she had some "sad news” about the star - who was not present in the ballroom and pre-recorded a package for fans. 

“Jeannie Mai had emergency surgery last night and she's in recovery. She's doing well. However, she's had to withdraw from this competition. 

“And she shared her emotional story from her hospital room. She did that for you. She really wants you to see that,” the ANTM alum said with a serious face. 

As Jeannie laid in her hospital bed the day she had to drop out of the show, the entertainer admitted she was "absolutely devastated that my journey on Dancing with the Stars has to end this way.”

She continued: “I found out that I have a throat abscess, where my tonsils and my throat had gotten so infected that it started permeating to the rest of the areas behind my neck and my ears that I had to get emergency surgery… which is why I'm here now.”

Footage is then shown of the reality star being wheeled into the procedure room by a team of nurses. 

It then flashes to a clip of Jeannie appearing tired in bed, but told by her doctor that “everything went great” in the surgery. 

"If you had waited one more day, your throat would have closed up," the doctor said of the severity of her condition. 

After the footage was played, cameras panned back to the ballroom as Tyra stood in tears and barely able to speak. 

As her voice trembled, she said: “Jeannie you will always be a part of this family, this dancing family. 

“Until the very last episode, you will be here,” she said as tears streamed down her face. 

She then gave props to Jeannie’s pro dance partner, Brandon Armstrong. 

“And to Brandon, you’re the best partner. You’re the most mature baby-faced man and have been so great every step of the way,” the host added. 

During today's Good Morning America, Jeannie confirmed she was out of the dance competition show after being rushed to the hospital.

She said in a statement that read: "My doctors discovered a health concern with my throat which requires immediate attention and surgery.
