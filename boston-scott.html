Boston Scott is the running back for the Philadelphia Eagles of the NFL and a professional Rocket League esports player of Dignitas. He signed a multi-year deal with Dignitas, a professional esports organization, as a content creator and substitute for their Rocket League roster.

Scott has been playing football since a young age. He was a part of his college football team, Louis Tech, where he rushed for 937 yards on 163 attempts for 5.7 yards per attempt in the regular season. 

The New Orleans Saints drafted Scott in the sixth round of the 2018 NFL Draft. On May 10, 2018, Scott signed his rookie contract with the Saints but, unfortunately, was waived on September 5, 2018, after making it into the initial 53-man roster. 

Scott was signed off the Saints practice squad by the Philadelphia Eagles on December 10, 2018. He scored his first professional touchdown on a 4-yard run against Buffalo Bills on October 27, 2019. 

His team, the Eagles, will play Super Ball LVII against the Kansas City Chiefs on February 12, 2023, in State Farm Stadium in Glendale, Arizona.

Early Life, Family & Education

Boston Scott was born on April 27, 1995, in Baton Rouge, Louisiana, to supporting parents Anthony Scott and Shelly Scott. He grew up with his older brother, Tony, and a sister Alana.

When Scott was in elementary school, his parents divorced, and he lived with his mother and siblings.

Dealing with his parent’s divorce at such a young age was very unfortunate, but the situation could not bring his passion and drive down.

Scott was always passionate about football and used to play in school. He went to Zachary High School in Zachary, Louisiana. 

After graduating high school, Scott went to Louisiana Tech University and played for the college football team for four seasons.

Career

Scott went to Zachary High School in Zachary, Louisiana. He was popular in high school for his extraordinary gameplay, which helped him earn local and state honors. Likewise, Scott did weightlifting in high school and was a state champion.

After graduating from high school, Scott went to Louisiana Tech University and became a part of their football team.

He played four seasons for the Louisiana Tech Bulldogs football team, averaging 937 yards on 163 attempts for 5.7 yards per attempt on a regular season average. 

Despite having a short stature, Scott scored well in his senior season. Because of his exceptional performance during the seasons, Scott was a candidate for Wuerffel Trophy. 

NFL Draft

The New Orleans Saints drafted Scott in the sixth round of the 2018 NFL Draft. On May 10, 2018, Scott signed his rookie contract with the Saints but, unfortunately, was waived on September 5, 2018, after making it into the initial 53-man roster. 

The Philadelphia Eagles signed Scott from the practice squad on December 10, 2018. 

On August 31, 2019, the Eagles released Scott in the final roster cuts but were resigned from the practice squad the next day. 

He scored his first professional touchdown on a 4-yard run on October 27, 2019, when the Eagles defeated the Bills 31-13. 

Scott’s performance from the last season earned him the backup running back position for 2020. He made a game-winning touchdown from Carson Wentz in a 22-21 win against the Giants on October 22, 2020. 

On April 1, 2021, he signed a one-year exclusive-rights free agent tender with the Eagles. Likewise, Boston extended his stay with the Eagles on March 18, 2022.

Rocket League

At first, Scott started playing Rocket league as a hobby with his friends. The game was a stress reliever and an escape from reality for the NFL player.

Eventually, he developed a passion and competitiveness for the game while playing and mastered the skills required. 

On February 3, 2022, Scott signed with professional esports organization Dignitas as a content creator and substitute for the Rocket League roster.

Injuries

Scott contracted the Coronavirus and was placed on the COVID list on January 3, 2022, by the officials. But he recovered after a week and was back in the game on January 10, 2022. 

On August 4, 2022, Scott suffered a cranial head concussion during a practice match. He was out of the game for two weeks.
