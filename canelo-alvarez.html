Early Life & Family

Santos Saul Alvarez Barragan professionally Canelo Alvarez was born in Guadalajara, Mexico, to parents Santos Alvarez and Ana Maria Barragan.

Although his family is originally from Los Reyes, Michoacán later moved to their current home at Juanacatlán, Jalisco.

Canelo was born as the youngest son in a big family of eight children, seven of them boys and one sister.

All Alvarez boys used to be very athletic since they were a kid. Among them, the older brother Rigoberto Alvarez first decided to take up boxing and became an example to his siblings.  

Canelo followed in the footsteps of his older brother, Rigoberto, and got into boxing. Additionally, two of his older brothers are also welterweight boxers, Ramon Alvarez and Ricardo Alvarez.

Alvarez first got in the ring at the age of 13, and since then, boxing has become an inseparable part of his life. As an amateur, Canelo participated in many boxing matches and even won the Junior Mexican National Championships twice in Mexico.

Canelo won his first gold medal before making his professional debut at the early age of 14. When Alvarez was just 20 years old, he also became the runner-up at WBC World Championship.

Boxing Career

Shortly after winning the gold medal in 2005, Canelo started playing professionally. Generally, amateur boxers don’t make their debut at age 15, but Canelo made the jump simply because he was very talented and no amateur fighters wanted to fight him.

Though he was fighting professional boxers much older than him, he knocked out most of his opponents and kept his winning streak.

In his first 19 months as a professional, he knocked out 11 of his 13 opponents, all of whom were significantly older than him.

Canelo started with several loud victories, and in 2008, he knocked out two of his rivals and won the prestigious ‘WBA Fedecentro” welterweight title. He continued his winning spree, and again in 2009, he won the ‘NABF’ and the ‘WBO Latino welterweight titles, defeating Mexican boxer Antonio Fitch. 

After that, Canelo competed in light middleweight in 2010 and won the WBC silver title. That same year, he also became the first boxer to knock out Carlos Baldomir.

Later that year, on September 14, the Mexican boxer suffered a massive loss against Floyd Mayweather Jr, marking the first loss of his professional career.

However

The loss could not take away the fighting spirit of Canelo as he went on to win many more fights and got victories over Alfredo Angulo in 2014.

He also successfully defended the title against British boxer Amir Khan on May 7, 2016. 

On September 15, 2018, Canelo fought in a rematch against Gennady Golovkin and won with a majority decision.

Initially, the fight was first scheduled to occur on May 5, 2018, but Álvarez tested positive for an illegal substance and was suspended for six months.

In his next match in 2019, Canelo competed with US boxer Daniel Jacobs on May 4, and won the ‘IBF middleweight title and retained the ‘WBA (Super),’ ‘The WBC,’ ‘The Ring,‘ and the lineal middleweight titles.

Not to mention, in his professional career, Canelo’s record currently stands at 58 wins, 2 losses, and 2 draws. In those 58 wins, 39 were by KO.

Net Worth And Income

One of the greatest Mexican boxers, Canelo Alvarez, has been active as a professional boxer since 2005. In 18 years, Alvarez has earned a lot of recognition from all over the world. 

Well, it’s not only the name and fame Canelo has earned throughout these years. He has pocketed millions of dollars in earnings, making him one of the highest-earning athletes of the year 2022, along with soccer stars Lionel Messi, Cristiano Ronaldo, and Neymar.

As of 2023, Alvarez has earned an estimated net worth of $180 million, per Forbes. Most of his fortune came from accumulated cash earnings from his successful boxing career and winning world-class championships.

In 2018, Canelo signed an 11-fight worth $365 million contract with Oscar De La Hoya‘s company, Golden Boy Promotions, and DAZN.

Personal life And kids

The undisputed super middleweight champion, Canelo’s love life is very active. In May 2021, Canelo married his longtime partner Fernanda Gomez in a ceremony at the Guadalajara Cathedral in Guadalajara, Mexico.

The duo reportedly met for the first time in 2017 at a charity gala and instantly hit off. After dating for about a year, they welcomed their first child together, Maria Fernanda Alvarez, in 2018.

Before Gomez, Canelo had plenty of relationships with famous ladies. However, he did not get married to any of them but had kids with them.

Girlfriends & Kids 

Alvarez has four kids in total, all from different women. While dating his school sweetheart Karen Beltrán, he welcomed his first daughter Emily Cinnamon Alvarez when he was just 17 in 2007. 

Soon after their daughter’s birth, Canelo and Karen separated. But he didn’t walk out of Emily’s life and maintained a good relationship with her. 

His second daughter Mia Ener Alvarez was with model Valeria Quiroz. Sadly, they also got separated after some time, and Mia lives with her mother in Los Angeles. 

Similarly, after dating for a year with his business partner Nadia Sepulveda, Canelo welcomed his third child (and the first boy), Saul Adiel Alvarez.

It was confirmed when Canelo publicly posted Saul’s picture on Instagram, captioning it “My boy #teamcanelo.” But, unfortunately, just like his other relationship, this relationship also didn’t work out, and Canelo and Nadia got separated after some time.

Besides these beauties, the boxer also has a dating history with other famous girls, Kate del Castillo (an actress), Shannon de Lima, Cynthia Rodriguez, and Marisol Gonzalez (2003 Miss Mexico Universe).
