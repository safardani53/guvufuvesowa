JIM Bob and Michelle Duggar gifted their son Jed with a $365,000 Arkansas home amid his engagement to Katey Nakatsu.

The Sun can exclusively reveal Jed, 22, listed a three-bedroom, three-bathroom Springdale, Arkansas home as his address for a November 2020 business license. 

Property records obtained by The Sun reveal the home that sits on over five acres of land is currently owned by his parents.  

Jim Bob, 55, and Michelle, 54, bought the property on March 30, 2020 for $365,000. 

According to Realtor, the 2,180 sq-ft home boasts an open-floor plan, brick fireplace, double ovens, patio, two-car garage and more amenities. 

The Counting On patriarch, who has a real estate empire worth millions, is known for gifting his children homes when they tie the knot.

Jim Bob and Michelle bought a home for $30,000 in December 2010 that they later gave to Joy-Anna and her husband Austin Forsyth in 2019 for just $1.

Jim Bob sold his son Justin, 18, a mobile home for $1 on December 8, 2020.

He bought the Springdale, Arkansas home on May 18, 2020 for $35,500. 

Justin ended up flipping the home and selling it for $195,000.

Jim Bob and Michelle’s son Josh, his wife Anna and their six children live in a warehouse on the Duggar family property. 

Joe and Kendra also live on the compound, but they instead reside in a stunning log cabin. 

Jed had been dating and proposed to Katey, whose real name is Katelyn, in secret, as their engagement photos and invitation to their virtual wedding set for April 3, 2021 leaked online. 

Last Fall, Jed confirmed that he “had a lady” and painted a pumpkin with the name “Katey” written on the front, though their engagement still hasn’t been announced by the family.

The Sun previously reported Jed’s future in-laws sold their Arizona home for $840,000 on July 1, 2020 and relocated to Arkansas amid the engagement.

Her parents, Kory and Kerry, bought a Springdale, Arkansas mansion for $594,900 on December 30, 2020. 

The home has an open-floor plan on the first floor, and boasts four bedrooms and four bathrooms.

The mansion has hardwood floors, high ceilings, and molding throughout the spacious living room, dining room and family room. 

The family room features a cozy stone fireplace. 

The kitchen features granite countertops, stainless steel appliances, a kitchen island and another island complete with bar stools. 

The backyard is complete with a huge in-ground pool and water fountain, pool house, outdoor kitchen, patio area and fireplace. 

Jed rented a three-bedroom, two-bathroom Springdale, Arkansas home with his twin brother, Jeremiah, in September 2019 for $950 a month. 

The Sun previously reported Jed moved out of the home after his landslide loss in the Arkansas State Representative race, as the home was listed for rent in December 2020. 

Jed’s former neighbor told The Sun: "He moved out after he lost the election. He was a quiet guy and not around here for very long. He went back to the family home.”

In November, Jed lost his bid for State Representative of District 89 to Democrat incumbent Megan Godfrey.

Jed lost by almost 700 votes, with the reality star getting 43 percent to Megan's 57 percent.

Jed’s campaign was controversial, as he was accused of "accepting illegal donations."
