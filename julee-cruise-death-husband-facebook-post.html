MUSICIAN Julee Cruise's husband has revealed the heartbreaking final thing he did for her before her death on Thursday aged 65.

Edward Grinnan confirmed his wife's death in a Facebook post in which he said she was now "at peace."

He also revealed that during her final moments, he played the song "Roam" for his wife.

The song, performed by the band B-52, was a smash hit in 1990 reaching the third spot on the Billboard Hot 100 chart.

Cruise, who disclosed that she suffered from the auto-immune disease lupus in 2018, performed with B-52 occasionally.

She would fill in for Cindy Wilson, who was a singer and one of the founding members of the band, IMDB reported.

"For those of you who go back, I thought you might want to know that I said goodby to my wife, Julee Cruise, today," Grinnan wrote.

"She left this realm on her own terms. No regrets. She is at peace.

"Having had such a varied music career she often said that the time she spent as a B filling in for Cindy while she was having a family was the happiest time of her performing life," he continued.

"She will be forever grateful to them. When she first stepped up to the mic with Fred and Kate she said it was like joining the Beatles. She will love them always and never forget their travels together around the world.

" I played her Roam during her transition. Now she will roam forever. Rest In Peace, my love, and love to you all."

Cruise went on to have her own success in the music industry.

She released four albums, from 1989 to 2011, and is best known for the song "Falling" which was used as the theme song for the show "Twin Peak," multiple outlets reported.

She was also known for her working relationship with David Lynch, the man behind Twin Peaks, and was often regarded as his muse.

Cruise featured on the show as a bar singer, and had her music included in the show and its soundtrack, Variety reported.

She was also featured on Twin Peaks' long-awaited third season in 2017.

And she performed on "Saturday Night Live" in 1990 when Sinéad O’Connor decided to protest against the Andrew Dice Clay hosting that weekend.

Cruise's influence on the music industry has been widespread, from her unique voice to her acting credits, Variety reported.

She covered Elvis Presley’s "Summer Kisses, Winter Tears" for Wim Wenders’ Until the End of the World movie soundtrack and worked alongside Nicolas Cage and Laura Dern in Industrial Symphony No 1, which was directed by Lynch, The Guardian reported.
