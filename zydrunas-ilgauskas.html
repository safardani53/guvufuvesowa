Atletas Kaunas, Cleveland Cavaliers, and Miami Heat were the team he played on before retiring in 2011. 

His strength was accurate jump shots and excellent rebounding, and Zydrunas was known for overcoming challenges during the game. 

He is one of the wealthiest former basketball players having a whopping net worth of over $50 million as of December 2023.

10,976 points, 6,191 rebounds, and 1,327 were the best statistics of his career. Likewise, he had an average score of 13.0 points, 7.3 rebounds, and 1.6 blocks per game.

Unfortunately, he faced many foot injuries and surgeries during his time in the NBA. Ilgauskas missed many games in his early career due to injury obstacles.

Early Life, Education & Family

Even though Zydrunas Ilgauskas is one of the most popular basketball players in the world, he hasn’t shared anything deep regarding his early life on the internet.

However, before making his debut on the NBA team, he played for his local team in his birthplace Kaunas. In fact, the name of the team was Atletas Kaunas. 

From 1993 to 1976, he contributed to his local team. 20.3 points, 12.8 rebounds, and 2.8 blocks per game were his average score when he played for Atletas Kaunas. 

He also had to face foot and ankle injuries during his early life. Due to that, Ilgauskas spent the entire season on the injured list in the 1996-97 season. After then, he made a debut in his professional career.

On the other hand, he went to Saint Ignatius High School. Unfortunately, he hasn’t updated anything regarding his college. 

Zydrunas Ilgauskas is son of Mecislovas llgauskas and Antranina llgauskiene. He is the only son without any siblings in his family.

Career

Zydrunas Ilgauskas was one of the most popular basketball players globally. He has represented himself in the two international teams and the national teams.

Professional Career 

The Cleveland Cavaliers selected Ilgauskas in the 1996 NBA draft as the 20th overall pick, with whom he signed a multi-year contract. 

In the 1997-98 season, he was rewarded for being the most valuable player of the Rookie Challenge during the All-Star Weekend. 

Zydrunas signed a vast extension contract in 1998 with the Cavaliers for over six years, valued at over $70 million. 

But sadly, again, Ilgauskas had to face another injury of the fractured navicular bone in his left foot on January 26, 2000. 

He returned and had an average score of 17.2 points and 7.5 rebounds in the 2002-03 season and was named an NBA all-star award.

Though he made a significant contribution to the team, it was recorded as the third-worst record in the team’s history. 

Contract Extension

On July 12, 2005, Ilgauskas again signed a five-year deal extension contract with the Cavaliers. In fact, he spent almost 14 years on the same team.  

He signed his last contract with the Cavaliers on March 23, 2010. By playing against the New Orleans Hornets, he made a great win over his opponent. 

In honor of the Lithuanian, Quicken Loans Arena was named “The Z” for a day. At the end of his contract with the Cavaliers, he became the third European player honored by an NBA team. 

After that, Miami Heat was the next team of Ilgauskas. He joined the Heat due to the influence of his teammate Lebron James and played briefly from July 17, 2010, to September 2011 

Having good players like Ilgauskas and James, they successfully reached the final of the 2011 NBA but couldn’t make up for the trophy.

National Team Career 

In 1994, Ilgauskas made a debut with the national team of Lithuania. He took the team to the qualifying spot in the EuroBasket in 1995.

The athlete got an average score of 7.7 points and seven rebounds per game. Although he was too eager to play for the national team in the 2008 Summer Olympics, the Cavaliers refused him to participate due to his history of injury. 

In 2008, he also attended a press conference meeting where he thanked everyone, including the federation, for their efforts and determination. 

Post Playing Career

After playing on a team with Cleveland Cavaliers, he was again hired by the Cavaliers’ GM, Chris Grant to serve as his assistant.

Moreover, on September 18, 2015, he joined Saint Ignatius High School as a coach for the boys’ team.

Net Worth, Salary, And Career Earnings

Since Zydrunas Ilgauskas spent 18 years of his life playing basketball, hence, he has a huge net worth of $55 million as of December 2023. 

By playing with Cleveland Cavaliers from 1996 to 2010, he succeeded in earning most of his wealth. Moreover, he also signed a contract worth $2.7 million with Miami Heat. 

When he played in the NBA, he had an estimated income of $125 million. It also includes the extra signing bonus from the signing team. 

With his massive salary, he was able to invest in real estate. In 2012, Ilgauskas listed his Miami Beach Sunset Island for $8.3 million.

The 5,166 square foot home, including the six-bathroom and five-bedroom, was sold through his associate Danny Hertzberg who helped him earn some extra income. 

Relationship And Personal Life

During the summer of 2004, Ilgauskas tied the knot with his lovely wife, Jennifer Ilgauskas. She was the owner of Darvo Medical Rebound Physical Therapy. 

After three years of getting married, they were compelled to lose a set of twins due to pregnancy complications.

It resulted in infants being born four months premature. They were so frustrated and sad during that period.

Later, they decided to adopt children in 2009; two Lithuanian brothers from his hometown, Kaunas. One was four years old, and another was five years old.

His wife passed away on September 11, 2022, but the cause of death was not revealed. 

In 2014, Zydrunas became a permanent citizen of the United States, resulting in him losing his Lithuanian citizenship.
