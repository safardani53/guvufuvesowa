The 43-year-old insists "your sex life gets better as you get older" and she's not alone. 

We reveal the saucy celebs who say bonking is better with age.

Sharon Stone

When the Basic Instinct star turned 55 she insisted that sex improved with age because women took more time between the sheets.

Sharon, now 65, said: “We understand our bodies and there's a blossoming. We're hungry for gourmet meals instead of fast food."

Salma Hayek

The Mexican actress frolics fulsomely in her fifties thanks to the joys of marriage.

Salma, 56, who married French businessman François-Henri Pinault, 60, in 2009, said: “Sex is not the key to a happy marriage, but it’s a side effect.

Rachel Bilson

The star of The OC didn’t enjoy an orgasm during sex until she was aged 38.

Rachel, 41, said: "It didn't happen for me until I was about 38. Isn't that crazy? It can be a challenge for some women. It had to do with me knowing my body.”

She revealed in a podcast this year that she now has a far better idea of what gets her going.

Rachel, who has a daughter with former boyfriend Hayden Christensen, explained: "I’ve learned that if you want to have an orgasm during sex, you have to be on top.”

Jane Fonda

The 85-year-old Oscar winning star has spoken about the joys of later years sex.

Last year during a radio interview Jane, who is currently starring in a new film called Book Club: The Next Chapter, said: “Women, I think, tend to get better because they lose their fear of saying what they need.”

She continued: “We waste way too much time not wanting to say: ‘Wait a minute, hold it, hold it, no, no, no. Slow down, and a little to the left.’ We don’t want to do that.

“But when we get older it’s like: ‘No, I know what I want. Give me what I want.’”

Danielle Lloyd

The former Miss Great Britain told The Sun last year that she was making time for intimacy despite having five children.

Danielle, 39, who appeared on Celebrity Big Brother, said: “I've never felt better about my body than I have done in the last couple of years, and I think that's made our sex life a lot better. 

“I like to be adventurous and have fun with sex. When I haven't felt in my best shape and I've had problems with my boobs after surgery, I've not felt as sexy. Now that's all sorted, I feel good about myself.”

The model, who has been married to husband Michael O'Neill since 2019, added: “As you get older, you know what to do, what you like and, if you've been with someone a long time, what your partner likes as well.”

Jenny Powell

The Greatest Hits Radio host told Fabulous Magazine that love making was more fulfilling in her fifties.

The 55-year-old brunette said: “Sex in your fifties gets better and better.

"Let's face it, when you know someone so well and you've been through so many journeys, it's just the best way to relax and enjoy each other.”

Jenny, who has been with boyfriend Martin Lowe for 12 years, added: “I strongly believe that you have to keep making the effort with each other, or it's a slippery slope.

“And my one golden rule for maintaining intimacy in a relationship is - don’t have a TV in your bedroom.”

Coleen Nolan

The Loose Women panellist got her mojo back after divorce left her lacking self-esteem.

The 58-year-old singer was very forward when tackling the subject of “Is intimacy better later in life” on the daytime show.

Coleen answered emphatically: "Yes, the answer is yes."

The mum-of-three commented: "If you're with the right person, it's the best thing ever."

She continued: "Maybe also now, this is it, take it or leave it. I've clearly taken it, and I feel in a much better place."

Denise Welch

The star of Loose Women has raved about the rampant nookie she enjoys with her ‘toy boy’ husband.

Actress Denise, 64, has been married to 50-year-old painter Lincoln Townley for a decade.
