Lost Mother To Cancer | Family & Early Life

Jason Preston, born on August 10, 1999, in Orlando, Florida, faced a tragic loss when his mother, Judith Sewell, passed away from lung cancer during his junior year in high school.

His father was absent from his life, leaving Preston to cope with his loss on his own.

Preston’s love for basketball was inspired by his mother, who would watch Detroit Pistons games and have her son sit with her.

Following his mother’s passing, Preston’s legal guardians became his aunt and uncle, Justin and Denise Morgan, who resided in Jamaica.

Since he was living in the United States, he would later move to live with his mother’s best friend Barbara Whittaker and her sons Brian-Marc and Russell Whittaker.

Whittaker knew Preston’s mother since 1984, when they both worked at a local high school in Kingston, Jamaica.

During their time there, Barbara was the head of the English department, and Jason’s mother was an economics teacher.

Preston’s favorite basketball players growing up were Tayshaun Prince and LeBron James.

Struggle To Find Collegiate Basketball Offers

Jason Preston attended William R. Boone High School in Orlando, Florida, where he only averaged two points per game during his two-year tenure.

As he struggled to find a college that would accept him due to his poor basketball performance, he decided to pursue a degree in journalism at the University of Central Florida.

Preston had also previously written stories about the Detroit Pistons for a website. He remembers his first article centered on Reggie Jackson‘s potential to have a breakout season in 2017.

However, he caught the eye of a prep coach during an Amateur Athletic Union tournament and joined Believe Prep Academy in Athens, Tennessee, where he made significant progress.

Preston averaged twelve points, nine assists, and seven rebounds per game, leading his team to an impressive 31-8 record.

Alumnus Of Ohio University

By creating and uploading his own highlights video on Twitter, he received offers from Ohio and Longwood and ultimately committed to play for Ohio Bobcats.

In his first year with Ohio, Jason was recognized for his promising performance by being named to the MAC All-Freshman Team.

He went on to play and start in all 32 games in his second year, averaging 16.8 points, 6.4 rebounds & 7.4 assists per game.

As a junior, the future NBA star scored his career-high 31 points against Illinois on November 27, 2020. He was named First Team All-MAC and MAC Tournament MVP.

NBA Career Of Jason Preston

Jason Preston, the 33rd overall pick (round-two) of Orlando Magic in the 2021 NBA Draft, decided to forgo his senior year at college to play in the NBA.

Subsequently, he was traded to Los Angeles Clippers, where he would join the experienced rosters like Paul George, Kawhi Leonard, and Marcus Morris Sr., among others.

Unfortunately, during his rookie preseason, he suffered an injury to his right foot after landing on another player’s shoe.

He underwent surgery on October 7, 2021, to repair ligament damage, causing him to miss the entire 2021-22 season.

Preston made his NBA debut for the Clippers in the 2022-23 season, playing against the Phoenix Suns on October 23, 2022.

In addition to major league, he plays for the Clippers’ G-league affiliate Ontario Clippers. He scored his G-league career-high 29 points on January 1, 2023, in a loss to Sioux Falls Skyforce.
