MADONNA was ripped by fans as she was accused of recreating Marilyn Monroe’s death scene.

The 63-year-old is facing backlash for her new naked photos.

In the latest V Magazine cover interview, Madonna was seen laying face down on a mattress with her bottom clearly exposed.

An additional shot showed prescription pill bottles could on a nearby nightstand.

Fans believe the photo is a reenactment of Marilyn’s tragic death.

The iconic actress was found in dead in her Brentwood, California home in 1962 and is believed to have overdosed on sedative drugs.

Shortly after Madonna’s photoshoot came out, fans were quick to note that the photo in bed resembled Marilyn on her deathbed.

Many rushed to Twitter to express their outrage over the photos, as pop culture expert Mike Sington tweeted: “For some morbid and eerie reason, Madonna decides to re-create Marilyn Monroe’s death bed.”

Another added: “Yuck. Gross and inappropriate.”

A following person commented: “Must be sad to still need so much attention. And this is so gross.”

PHOTOGRAPHER'S INSPIRATION

The photographer for Madonna’s shoot was inspired by Bert Stern's series The Last Sitting, which is believed to be the final photoshoot that Marilyn participated in.

The photos were taken just six weeks before her death on August 4, 1962 at the age of 36.

In the recent shoot, Madonna bared all as she posed topless and only wore a thong as she laid face down and had a cross on her back.

The Material Girl singer also showed off her cleavage while posing in a bustier as she was surrounded by male bodyguards.

NO HANDOUTS FOR LOURDES

The photoshoot comes just weeks after Madonna’s daughter, Lourdes Leon, revealed her mother has refused to give her any handouts despite being a millionaire.

While speaking to actress Debi Mazar for Interview magazine, the 25-year-old revealed:  “My mom is such a control freak, and she has controlled me my whole life. 

“I needed to be completely independent from her as soon as I graduated high school.”

Lourdes has proven she can support herself and has paid her own way through college and for her own apartment. 

She continued: “We don’t get any handouts in my family. Obviously, I grew up with extreme privilege. There’s no denying that.
