Newmarket trainer Martyn Meade eyeing big targets for 'maturing' Eminent

 Posted on July 27, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  341 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

AFTER falling just short in Group One company, Martyn Meade believes Eminent can finally fulfil his full potential as a four-year-old in 2018.

The son of Frankel started the season with a highly promising win in the Craven Stakes at Newmarket, an effort which was to be followed up with creditable performances in both the 2,000 Guineas and the Epsom Derby.

Eminent caused mayhem in a messy Coral Eclipse - he tried to bite Roger Charlton's Decorated Knight mid-race - before ending his campaign with a Group Two win in France and a respectable third in the Irish Champion Stakes at Leopardstown.

Meade has been vocal throughout the season in his belief that this Frankel colt can win at the very highest level, an opinion the Newmarket handler still clearly holds.

"He has been turned out for six weeks or so since his run in the Irish Champion. He has matured," said Meade.

"I will bring him back early next year and maybe just give him one run before the Prince of Wales's at Ascot. Hopefully he will really fulfil all his potential there.
