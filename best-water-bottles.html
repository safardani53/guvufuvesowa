Things to Consider Before Buying a Water Bottle

Material

Arguably the most important aspect to consider before buying a water bottle is the material. Most water bottles are made from one of three materials: stainless steel, plastic, or glass. Stainless steel is great for its insulation properties and durability. Plastic bottles tend to be lighter in weight and less expensive. However, it’s important to know whether or not the plastic is BPA-free since the chemical can seep into food or drinks being consumed from products that are made with this material, potentially causing negative health effects down the line, according to research conducted by the Mayo Clinic. Check the label of any plastic water bottle you’re considering to determine if it is BPA-free. Glass water bottles are easy to clean, but they are a bit more delicate and weigh more than their stainless steel and plastic counterparts. 

Insulation

Reusable water bottles should aim to not only reduce the amount of plastic that goes to waste but also retain the temperature of your drink — that is, if it's insulated. Many water bottles are constructed with double-wall vacuum insulation, which is ideal for keeping cold drinks freezing and hot coffee scalding. However, this often ups the price of a water bottle and isn't necessary for those who want to just take their water on the go. Plastic water bottles are just as common and often come at a cheaper price, providing you with the portability of your water, but only being available for use with cold drinks (do not mix hot beverages with plastic).

Lid Type

Another consideration when choosing a water bottle is the lid. This will determine how you open and close the bottle for refilling and cleaning as well as the type of drinking mechanism it has. Some lids have a larger opening for drinking, while others have smaller area to place your mouth. In addition, some water bottles come with several lids for different drinking methods, including chug, sip, and straw lids. 

Durability

It's pretty easy to drop your water bottle while on a hike or walking around, so it's important to go for a kind that doesn't dent or leak easily. Stainless steel and hard, BPA-free plastics are often the best options for this type of durability, a standout model being the Yeti Rambler 26 oz. Bottle after passing the drop test with flying colors. Grace Martinez, Rock 'n' Roll Run Club Leader of the PNW Ladies Running Group, finds durability to be paramount in the search for a worthwhile water bottle. "If I was also using [it for] a hike, it'd be nice to have a sturdy bottom for the times you set it down or a clip for the backpack," she shares, touching on the extra features that really upgrade a bottle.

Cleaning

Another factor to keep in mind when shopping for your new water bottle is the way in which your water bottle can be cleaned. Make sure to check if the bottle is dishwasher safe or not and to clean accordingly, as non-dishwasher safe plastics can melt when exposed to sterilizing heat. The cap and bottle may have different instructions as well, as many caps are not dishwasher friendly or can only be used on the top rack while the bottle can go on the bottom.

How We Tested

The PEOPLE Tested team evaluated each of the 26 water bottles based on drinkability, seal, portability, insulation, durability, ease of cleaning, and value. Each product was then evaluated on a scale of one to five, one being poor and five being excellent, based on our experience with the product. First, each person tried opening the caps and testing how easy it was to take a drink. They then tested the seal to determine if it was leak-proof or had a few spills. Next, we carried the bottle around, testing the handles and feeling how heavy or light each bottle felt while full of water. The bottles were then left for six hours to see if each one remained cold, testing the water with a thermometer before and after the six hours. Our testers then evaluated the bottles' durability, dropping them from waist height three times and assessing the damage (or lack thereof) afterwards. They then were shown the prices of each bottle and our testers gave their honest feedback on whether or not they felt the value was justified.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Is it better to drink from a glass or stainless steel water bottle?

    There are pros and cons to both types of water bottles when it comes to drinkability. Glass keeps water in its purest form, by not imparting any taste or chemicals. Stainless steel can sometimes leave a metallic taste, but it is also safe from chemicals. Another big advantage for stainless steel bottles is their ability to insulate cold or hot drinks for hours which glass cannot do.

  • Is it OK to drink water left overnight in a water bottle?

    Yes, if the bottle is clean and closed properly, it is safe to drink. However, you should not drink water from a disposable bottle that has been in a particularly hot environment, such as a car or the sun, for an extended period of time. In this case, chemicals can leach from the plastic into the water.

  • Can you freeze a water bottle?

    Freezing a water bottle can be a great way to ensure you'll have an ice-cold beverage for hours. Unfortunately, putting a metal water bottle in the freezer is not recommended. Reusable plastic or disposable bottles work better and will not release chemicals through the freezing process.

  • How do you clean a water bottle?

    You can and should clean your water bottle daily. Using warm water, a bit of dishwashing liquid, and a bottle brush, scrub the sides and bottom of the bottle as well as the lip. Then rinse thoroughly and dry with a clean dish towel or paper towel or let air dry with the lid off.
