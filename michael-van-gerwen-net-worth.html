Early Life

The Dutch professional was born on 25 April 1989 in Boxtel, Netherlands, to his parents, Henri van Gerwen and Wilma van Germen.

He grew up with three siblings. During his childhood, he was interested in soccer and used to play as a defender until he was 12.

Michael began playing darts at 13 and reached the final of the Primus Masters Youth event at 14. He then continued his winning streak and amassed youth titles in 2005, including the German Open, German Gold Cup, Norway Open, Northern Ireland Open, Swedish Open, and the Dutch National Youth Championship.

Career

As mentioned above, the Dutch dart player started to get into darting at 13 and won a major dart title at 17.

Leading the dart world by storm, Gerwen made the record over several Open tournaments as a teenager.

In 2007, Gerwen defeated dart players like Raymond van Barneveld with a great hit on nine-dart. He also defeated Phil ‘The Power’ Taylor that same year straight.

Later, reaching two PDC Youth World Championship finals, he brought improvements and joy to experience a remarkable resurgence in his career. 

Moreover, with the pace of time, Michael won several championships and was named Young Player of the Year in 2011.

In his professional career, Gerwen has won several Pro Tour and the World Grand Prix titles with his best performances. He also had his Grand Slam of Darts three times.
