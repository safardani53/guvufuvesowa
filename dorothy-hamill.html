Career

When Dorothy participated and won the US Championship at the novice level in 1969, it marked her first achievement on a national scale.

In the junior US championship that year, Dorothy finished in second place. She began competing at the senior level in 1971. In 1974, Dorothy won the US championship, which she retained until 1976. She was given coaching by Carlo Fassi by the US Figure Skating Association when they saw her incredible potential. She received all the training she needed to compete on an international stage.

When she won the silver medal in the 1974 World Championship in Munich, Germany, she made significant progress in her career. She took home silver at the world competition in Colorado Springs the following year.

When she was 19 years old, she won the gold medal and took first place at the 1976 Winter Olympics in Innsbruck, Austria.

A well-known sports figure, Dorothy is adored by her followers all around the world. Between 1968 and 1969, she won the American Championship in the Amateur division. She has won the US National Championship three times (1974 to 1976).

Additionally participating in international tournaments, Dorothy took home the Nebelhorn Trophy. She held the top spot in the Richmond Trophy competition between 1972 and 1973.

Both the World Figure Skating Hall of Fame and the United States Figure Skating Hall of Fame have her name included. In her hometown of Greenwich, a skating rink bears her name. Dorothy Hamill Skating Rink is the new name of the rink.

Dorothy won the Winter Olympic Gold Medal in 1976, which was held in Austria. Additionally, she won the title of Ladies Singles World Champion in Sweden in 1976. In 1991 and 2000, she was admitted into the and subsequently the World Figure Skating Hall of Fame.
