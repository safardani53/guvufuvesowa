PGA Championship DELAYED due to frost after freezing weather warning with tee times in chaos

 Posted on March 16, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  229 words  |  Martina Birk

THE PGA CHAMPIONSHIP had to be DELAYED due to frosty conditions.

The 105th edition of the golf major was due to get underway at 7am local time (12pm BST) on Thursday.

But bitterly cold conditions brought a delay to proceedings in Pittsford, New York.

A practice round on Monday was pushed back due to frost.

And the conditions were even colder as the first players prepared to tee off.

Organisers later confirmed play would start at 8.50am local time (1.50pm BST).

Spaniard Rahm, 28, added to his 2021 US Open title with victory at The Masters last month.

Northern Ireland's Rory McIlroy, 34, is also in the hunt for the PGA Championship as he bids to end a nine-year wait for his fifth major.
