'Miracle' 6.95 Dream Cream from Lush 'cures' baby boy's agonising eczema in 10 hours

 Posted on May 31, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  800 words  |  Larita Shotwell

A 'MIRACLE' cream has helped 'cure' a baby boy's agonising eczema, his mum claims.

Unable to sleep and scratching until he bled, Cash Rudd was left in a hot mess by the painful skin condition.

Mum Sarah tried all sorts of lotions prescribed by doctors, and even stopped breastfeeding her five-month-old son fearing he might have an allergy.

But nothing seemed to help.

Now, the 36-year-old claims Lush's Dream Cream, which costs just £6.95, has eradicated Cash's eczema and cleared his skin up.

"The eczema started three or four weeks after he was born," Sarah said.

“He got very red around his forehead and had these angry sores come up. It spread to his scalp and would come down between his eyes.

“Even as a newborn he would be trying to claw at it. Everything we bought for him to wear came with scratch mittens.

“We found it would get a lot worse at night. He would scratch incessantly at his face and would rub his head against the side of his cot.

“He was a hot mess. He would just scratch and sweat, he was getting himself so upset.

“I would have to sit with him at night to try and keep his arms down.

“I would go to sleep holding his little hands because if he woke up he would just scratch. He didn’t sleep properly for four months.

“If you cuddled him he would rub his face against your clothes.”

Cosmetics clinic director Sarah and business owner hubby Tom, 33, first took Cash to the doctors about six weeks after he was born.

They saw doctors three times and were prescribed various creams, but nothing worked - not even hydrocortisone.

Then a dermatologist suggested trying a dairy-free diet.

But after switching to dairy-free formula, Sarah said it made no difference at all, if anything making it worse.

“In the end I put a shout out on social media saying I was desperate as he’d had it for four months and had not been sleeping properly," she said.

“I got about 180 responses and the Dream Cream kept coming up.

“We went out and bought some that day and put it on him that evening. Within ten hours it had completely gone – he was just left with the scratches.

“His skin was transformed and he’s not had a single flare up since. It really is dream cream.

“Lots of people who have psoriasis said it worked for them. It’s obviously got some really healing benefits.

“We bought the smallest tub which cost around £6 as I wasn’t sure whether it would work. We used a tiny bit and it goes quite far.

“It’s been an absolute miracle for him and it was instant. All of the redness and the lumps had gone and his skin was clear.

“We returned to the doctor so he could have his jabs and they couldn’t believe it.

“He doesn’t need it any more but I’m still putting it on as a precaution and because it makes his skin really nice and soft.

“I think it was invented by someone who suffered from psoriasis and having read the reviews it’s helped a lot of people with eczema.”

Sarah, who is also mum to Thalia, 11, Rocco, 9, Delilah, 4, Aria, 1, said Cash has now been transformed into a happy smiley baby.

Sarah said: “He was not a happy baby. He was fed up all the time.

“I remember we went to Center Parcs and there was a baby there who was around the same age and he was smiling. I said to my husband ‘I don’t think I’ve ever seen Cash smile’.

“It was not until we sorted his skin out that he smiled. He was so uncomfortable for such a long time. It made him miserable.

“He’s now completely different. He smiles all the time, he’s content and he’s a really easy baby. He’s sleeping through the night and he’s a lot calmer.”

Mark Constantine, Lush co-founder and managing director said: "For me it's what the whole business is about, to deliver effective product that does a little more than it says on the pot."
