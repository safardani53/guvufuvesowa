CELEBRITY Jeopardy!'s latest contestants have saved one category for last - and the internet was clueless as to why.

The contestants on the December 6 episode of Celebrity Jeopardy were Kyra Sedgwick, Mo Rocca, and Amanda Seales.

They faced off in the last quarterfinal game of the season, hosted by the illustrious Ken Jennings, 49.

During the Double Jeopardy! round, there was a category titled: "Also A Taylor Swift Song."

Although Taylor's fans would have jumped at the opportunity to choose that category, the celebrities did not.

It was the last category left on the board at the end of the round, and it was virtually untouched.

Questions in the category included: "Careful! This washing machine cycle using cold water and low spin speed is suggested for lingerie & silk neckties."

In the category, this question was worth the least amount of money, but none of the celebrities were able to answer it.

Yet, any swiftie will tell you, the answer was "Delicate."

'MASTERMINDS'

Fans of the show on X, formerly known as Twitter, couldn't fathom why Taylor's category was ignored.

One said: “They're avoiding the Taylor Swift category like the plague. Way to disrespect the newly minted Time Person of the Year."

“Why are we not playing the 'Also a Taylor Swift Song' category on Celeb Jeopardy???" asked another.

A third replied: “They kept putting it off and I couldn’t fathom why!"

Thankfully, the rest of the clues in the category didn't go as poorly, and journalist Mo Rocca wound up winning the game in a runaway.

He'll advance to the semifinals where an eventual $1M is up for grabs (for charity) and potentially, like this year, an invite to the annual Tournament of Champions where the prize is $250K (not for charity),

BOUNCED BACK

Starting in January, Celebrity Jeopardy! is getting bounced from its usual Wednesday night slot to Tuesdays on ABC.

The unexpected changeup was reported by Deadline and it comes as Celebrity Jeopardy! Season 2 is underway.

Starting on January 2, the celebrity edition of the game show will air on Tuesdays at 8 p.m. ET.

This comes as the network is eyeing a run for Hulu's Only Murders In The Building.
