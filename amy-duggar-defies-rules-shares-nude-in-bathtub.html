Fans have long considered Amy the rebellious one in the famous family as she often defies the rules.

Earlier this month, the mother-of-one donned sexy leopard-print swimwear during a pool day.

She wore another black bathing suit, displaying her cleavage while lounging by the water on another occasion as well.

There are several shots of her son Daxton on an amusement ride, drinking frozen lemonade and holding tickets.

In addition to the Duggar dress code, Amy has also been vocal about her feelings towards another family rule.

Recently, she took her son to a thrift store to "shop fall."

She posted a short video giving fans a tour of the fall-themed shelves at the store, with Halloween decorations scattered throughout.

The clip showed witches, skeletons, haunted lawn signs, and other scary decorations.

Amy didn't shy away or try to hide the Halloween decorations either.

At one point, she even panned over a pumpkin sign that read: "Happy Halloween."

The Duggar family has been outspoken about their decision not to celebrate Halloween.

Amy's cousin, Jinger, also married into a family that abstains from Halloween.

Jinger's husband, Jeremy Vuolo, disagrees with the "disturbing" and "Satanic origins" of the holiday.

But, he believes the holiday can be celebrated in a "pure" way.

Apparently, dressing up as a tiger for Halloween is pure because Jinger adorned an orange outfit and whiskers in 2021.

Proof of her costume was posted on social media in defiance of both her father and husband.

Sister Jill has also been known to celebrate the spooktacular holiday by decorating and dressing her kids in costume.
